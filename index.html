I am Julio Gutiérrez Moreno, PhD and Senior Researcher leading the Materials Science team at the Computer Applications in Science and Engineering (CASE) department of the Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC). In my research, I use atomistic simulation methods to study fundamental properties of materials. Most of my research is dedicated to the modelling and simulation at the atomic-scale, based on ab initio Density Functional Theory (DFT), beyond DFT methods and classical molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations, with a special focus on developing and testing novel methodologies for their efficient implementation on high-performance computing (HPC) platforms.

I did my PhD at the Department of Materials Science and Engineering at the University of Ioannina in Greece, and worked as Postdoctoral Researcher at the Materials Modelling for Devices group, in Tyndall National Institute (Republic of Ireland) and at the Institute for Advanced Study at Shenzhen University (People’s Republic of China).

I have created this website to keep it as a contact point with other researchers and potential collaborators. Moreover, I can also share the most recent outcomes of my current projects. In this way, all the material will be kept here and papers will be freely distributed after journal embargo period, without being subjected to publisher websites or changes of my research institute.

Research Projects

A brief overview of the projects, materials, and topics I engage with. Get in touch for further detail on any of these.

Ab Initio Simulations at the Exascale

The Materials Science Group at BSC-CASE participates in the NOMAD and MaX Centres of Excellence. Funded by the European Commission, these projects adapt ab initio computational methods for incoming exascale platforms. We contribute to benchmarking and development of codes including SIESTA, Abinit, exciting, FHI-aims and GPAW, and libraries such as ELPA.

NOMAD CoE MaX CoE HPC Exascale SIESTA

Data Science for Materials

Our team serves as one of the main HPC nodes of the Materials Commons for Europe initiative — a collaborative platform for sharing and managing materials science research data across the EU. Applying FAIR principles, it standardises how experimental and computational results are stored, accelerating discovery from next-generation alloys to sustainable energy solutions.

FAIR Data Materials Commons Open Science ML

Metal-Oxide Surfaces & 2D Nanosheets

Theory-guided screening and atomically precise synthesis for nanostructured materials in catalysis, batteries, and water depollution. I coordinate an EIG-Concert Japan project on machine-learning-driven design of atomically-layered heterostructures for green H₂ production, in collaboration with partners in Japan and Turkey.

Green H₂ Catalysis 2D Materials HfO₂ TiO₂

Fusion Materials & Low-Scaling Ab Initio

Standard DFT scales as O(N³). Linear-scaling algorithms allow doubling atom count with only double computation time. In the FusionCAT project we use the LS version of BigDFT to simulate neutron-irradiation defects in tungsten — the primary plasma-facing candidate material for fusion power — with ab initio accuracy at realistic scales.

BigDFT Tungsten Fusion FusionCAT LS-DFT

Antifouling Coatings for Water Sensors

In the UisceSense project, theoretical methods simulate biomolecule adsorption at modified surfaces to develop new coatings preventing biofouling on water sensor surfaces. Research covered TiN-TiO₂ interfaces, surfaces, and neonicotinoid sensing for real-time detection of hazardous substances in natural water sources.

Biofouling TiN–TiO₂ SERS Water Quality

Low-Rigidity Ti Alloys

Developing a computational procedure from ab initio toward large-scale molecular dynamics for investigating and designing low-rigidity β-type Ti-based alloys for orthopaedic implants. Studied structural, electronic, and mechanical properties of TiNb alloys, as well as ternary systems TiNbIn, TiNbSn, and TiNbHf.

TiNb Alloys Biomedical β-phase Elasticity MD
